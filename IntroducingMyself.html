Introducing Myself: Luke Opperman

April 6, 2021

All over the stack developer, parent and co-explorer with a 4 year old.

Goals

  • Meaningful ethical projects and environment
  • Substitute worker-owned team for independent consulting income
  • Balanced with parenting, gardening, woodworking, travel

Working History

  • 15 years in startup and small companies
  • Finance, Marketing, Retail
  • Team lead and developer/product management
  • Mostly product or platform growth
  • Using open source tools, varying commitments to contribution
  • 10 years in consulting and independent contracting
  • Healthcare, Environment, Education, Equity
  • Client-facing project managing
  • Mostly data management, manipulation, and presentation
  • Project-based, flexible part-time schedule

Technologies

  • Started with Linux, Perl, flavors of LAMP
  • Python and Postgresql for a long time, page-driven backend integration into APIs
  • Now Node.js APIs and Backbone to WebComponents &  React as much of backend MVC implementation has moved towards SPA and modular client side.
  • XP into Agile project cycle, short term story-driven commitment and frequent releases, with open-source project influenced use of feature branches, test-driven, pull requests.
  • Problem discovery and decomposition, architecture description, feature design proposals, and code review for team cohesion and communication.
  • Integration of a growing number of secondary data stores, often Mongo, Redis, ElasticSearch, bound to job queues and event streams.
  • Nearly all cloud & container hosting these days, Heroku & AWS & GCP.

Limitations

  • Sales and networking are so hard for me. I’ve often been involved in sales and client management, but always comfortably as the realist technologist hedging our abilities once a proposal is in the works.
  • Currently very part-time capacity, even if I close out my existing contract responsibilities.
  • Recalcitrant to identify needs and product ideas, would rather problem solve and shape design to a need.

Place In Co-op Space

  • Interested in community and democratic organizations for a long time, but no real experience.
  • I see other co-ops organized around:
  • Worker-owned custom development consulting agency
  • Product development and associated services
  • Implementation/hosting agency for specific technology
  • Open/Ethical Source umbrellas
  • But other than continuing to reproduce small consultancies developing tools for non-technical organizations, I don’t have a clear direction.
